Executive Employment Agreement

Chief Executive Officer (CEO) Grade
Belgium Jurisdiction

This Executive Employment Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into and made effective as of 01/06/2026, by and between:


1. The Employer:

Ariolas BV, a company incorporated and organized under the laws of Belgium, with its registered office situated at Rue de la Loi 16, 1000 Brussels, registered with the Crossroads Bank for Enterprises (KBO/BCE) under number 0799.123.456, and represented herein by its Board of Directors (the "Company").

2. The Executive:

Joe Lernout, residing at Avenue Louise 250, 1050 Brussels, Belgium (the "Executive").

The Company and the Executive are hereinafter collectively referred to as the "Parties" and individually as a "Party".

Article 1: Position and Duties

The Executive is employed in the position of Chief Executive Officer (CEO). In this capacity, the Executive shall report directly to the Board of Directors of the Company and shall have the duties, responsibilities, and authority standard for a Chief Executive Officer under Belgian corporate law. The Executive agrees to devote the whole of their professional time, attention, and energies to the performance of these duties.

Article 2: Term and Commencement

This Agreement is concluded for an indefinite duration. The Executive's employment under this Agreement shall commence on 01/06/2026 (the "Commencement Date").

Article 3: Place of Work & Exempt Status

The Executive's primary place of work will be the Company's registered office, currently located in Brussels. However, the nature of the duties requires regular travel within Belgium and internationally.

The Parties acknowledge that the Executive qualifies as "person invested with a leading position or a position of trust" (*personne investie d'un poste de direction ou de confiance / personen bekleed met een leidinggevende functie of een vertrouwenspost*) within the meaning of the Belgian Act of 16 March 1971 on employment. Consequently, statutory limitations on working hours and corresponding allowances do not apply to this employment.

Article 4: Remuneration and Benefits

As consideration for the performance of their duties, the Executive shall receive:

  • Base Salary: A gross monthly base salary of €18,500, payable in accordance with the standard payroll practices in Belgium (including 13.92-month structural standards if applicable).
  • Variable Compensation: An annual performance bonus of up to 40% of the annual base salary, subject to targets and KPIs determined exclusively by the Board of Directors.
  • Executive Benefits: A premium company vehicle, hospitalization insurance, group pension plan, representation allowance, and modern mobile/computational equipment in accordance with company policies.
Article 5: Confidentiality

The Executive acknowledges that during their employment, they will have access to highly confidential and proprietary information. The Executive agrees, both during the term of this Agreement and indefinitely thereafter, to maintain strict confidentiality regarding all such information and not to disclose it to any third party without express written permission.

Article 6: Non-Compete Covenant

In accordance with Article 86 of the Belgian Act of 3 July 1978 on employment contracts, the Executive agrees that upon termination of this Agreement for any reason, they shall not engage in any competitive enterprise, directly or indirectly, for a period of twelve (12) months. This restriction applies geographically to the territory of Belgium. In consideration of this non-compete obligation, the Company shall pay the Executive a monthly non-compete indemnity equal to fifty percent (50%) of the gross monthly salary for the duration of this covenant, unless the Company waives the non-compete covenant within fifteen (15) days of termination.

Article 7: Termination

Either party may terminate this Agreement by giving a written notice of termination. The length of the notice period shall be 12 months. The notice shall start in accordance with the rules laid down by Belgian statutory employment law. The Company reserves the right to terminate this contract immediately for serious cause (*dringende reden / motif grave*) without notice or compensation in lieu of notice.

Article 8: Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This Agreement is governed by, and shall be construed in accordance with, Belgian law. Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement that cannot be resolved amicably shall be submitted to the exclusive jurisdiction of the Labor Courts (*Tribunal du travail / Arbeidsrechtbank*) of Brussels, Belgium.
